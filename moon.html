THE STORY OF THE MOON

The Moon has always been a mysterious and beautiful object in the night sky. For thousands of years, people have looked up at it with curiosity, creating stories, myths, and legends about its origin and purpose.

How it formed

Scientists believe that the Moon was formed about 4.5 billion years ago after a massive collision between Earth and a Mars-sized object. The debris from that impact eventually came together to form the Moon we see today.

Its role on Earth

The Moon plays an important role in our planet. It controls the ocean tides and helps stabilize Earth's rotation. Without the Moon, life on Earth might be very different.

The Space Race

In the 1960s, the United States and the Soviet Union competed in the Space Race. After the Soviets sent the first human to space, Yuri Gagarin, the U.S. aimed higher to reach the Moon.

The first landing

On July 20, 1969, Apollo 11 successfully landed on the Moon. Neil Armstrong became the first human to step on its surface, saying the famous words: "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."

Why it matters

The Moon landing showed that humans could explore beyond Earth. Today, space agencies are planning to return to the Moon, making it an important part of future space exploration.
