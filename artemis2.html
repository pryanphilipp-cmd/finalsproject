ARTEMIS II: HUMANS RETURN TO LUNAR ORBIT

Artemis II will be the first crewed mission of NASA’s Artemis program. After the successful test flight of Artemis I, this mission will carry astronauts around the Moon for the first time in over 50 years.

The mission begins

Artemis II is planned to launch in the mid-2020s using the powerful Space Launch System (SLS). This mission will test all systems with humans onboard before attempting a lunar landing in future missions.

The crew

  • Reid Wiseman - Commander
  • Victor Glover - Pilot
  • Christina Koch - Mission Specialist
  • Jeremy Hansen - Mission Specialist

The journey

The crew will travel around the Moon and return to Earth without landing. This flight will test life-support systems, navigation, and deep space operations with astronauts onboard.

Why it matters

Artemis II is a crucial step toward landing humans on the Moon again. It will help ensure that future missions, like Artemis III, are safe and successful.

The future

This mission represents humanity’s return to deep space. It prepares the way for building a long-term human presence on the Moon and future journeys to Mars.
