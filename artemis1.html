RETURN TO THE MOON

After more than 50 years since the Apollo missions, NASA launched Artemis I to begin a new era of space exploration. This mission is the first step in returning humans to the Moon and eventually reaching Mars.

The mission begins

Artemis I launched on November 16, 2022, using NASA’s most powerful rocket, the Space Launch System (SLS). Unlike the Apollo missions, this flight did not carry astronauts—it was an uncrewed test mission.

The Orion spacecraft

The mission tested the Orion spacecraft, designed to carry humans farther into space than ever before. Orion traveled thousands of kilometers beyond the Moon to ensure it could safely support future crews.

Around the Moon

Artemis I successfully orbited the Moon and sent back stunning images of Earth and deep space. It demonstrated that modern technology is ready for long-distance space travel.

Safe return

After nearly a month in space, Orion safely returned to Earth on December 11, 2022, landing in the Pacific Ocean. The mission proved that the spacecraft could survive extreme heat during re-entry.

Why it matters

Artemis I is the beginning of a new chapter in space exploration. Future missions aim to land the first woman and the next man on the Moon, build a lunar base, and prepare humanity for missions to Mars.
